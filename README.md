
# HR Analytics Dashboard (Interactive Dashboard using Tableau)

## 📌 Project Objective  
To build an interactive HR analytics dashboard that helps HR professionals and leadership teams make data-driven decisions regarding employee demographics, attrition, performance, and satisfaction. The aim is to identify problem areas and propose actionable solutions for improving employee retention and organizational efficiency.

## 📂 Project File  
- [Download Tableau Dashboard File](./HR%20ANALYTICS%20DASHBOARD.twbx)

## ❓ Key Questions Addressed  
- What is the gender distribution in the company?
- What are the main reasons for employee attrition?
- Which departments and roles face the highest attrition?
- What is the average age and experience of employees across roles?
- Which job roles show the highest job satisfaction or dissatisfaction?
- How does performance rating relate to attrition?

## 📊 Dashboard Interaction  
- Visual filters and slicers for department, gender, job role, and attrition status
- Highlighted KPIs such as attrition rate, average satisfaction, and active employees
- Dynamic graphs for:
  - Attrition by department and job role
  - Age vs. Monthly Income
  - Satisfaction Level Distribution

## 🔄 Process  
- Data cleaning and preprocessing in Excel (null handling, formatting, transformation)
- Built calculated fields in Tableau for custom metrics (e.g., Attrition %, Avg. Age)
- Used bar charts, heatmaps, pie charts, and trendlines for effective storytelling
- Developed a user-friendly layout with clear titles and slicers for easy navigation

## 🔍 Key Insights  
- **Sales and R&D departments** have the highest attrition rate.
- **Job dissatisfaction** is notably high among **Sales Executives** and **Lab Technicians**.
- **Men aged 25–44** show higher attrition trends.
- **Job level and monthly income** have a strong correlation with retention.
- **Performance rating** doesn’t always align with attrition—some high performers still leave.

## ✅ Final Recommendations  
- Focus retention efforts on **Sales Executives** and **technical roles** with low satisfaction.
- Conduct exit interviews to analyze **dissatisfaction causes** and address them.
- Introduce **career development programs** for young employees (age 25–34).
- Promote flexible benefits and tailored incentives for **mid-career professionals**.
